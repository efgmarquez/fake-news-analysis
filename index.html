Here's an overview of our project.

Understand the motivation behind our project.

Motivation

Since 2016, there have been numerous mis/disinformation tweets regarding Marcos Sr.'s Last Will and Testament. In particular, tweets have been circulating, stating that Marcos Sr.'s wealth will be used for national and humanitarian projects, according to his last will, in an attempt to bring another Marcos into power. However, the late dictator's last will, in fact, specifically stated that all of his estate "of every nature and kind" will be bequathed to his wife and four children.

Problem

The spread of these misinformative tweets contribute to the rampant problem of information disorder in our country. Especially that these tweets propagate in a political nature, misinformation about powerful political images could greatly affect people's collective beliefs and the country's political landscape.

Solution

The potential implications of disinformation in the political landscape especially during elections make it imperative that these phenomena's seasons & clusters be observed. Hence, our group aims to analyze these misinformative tweets to understand the trends and patterns among them that could potentially help with fake news counter-propagation measures.

Problem Formulation

The first key problem: factors.

Research Question

Which factors that affect the spread of misinformation about Marcos's wealth and last will exhibit in terms of:

  1. linked personalities/institutions,
  2. complexity of language used,
  3. cited content as proof of the alleged last will?

Hypotheses

  1. The tweets which mention financial institutions such as the World Bank, and political personalities such as other Marcos relatives to support misinformation regarding Marcos's last will received more engagements than other tweets.
  2. The tweets which use shorter words and fewer characters to talk about Marcos's alleged last will received more engagement than long-form tweets.
  3. The tweets with attached media and links to support Marcos's supposed last will receive more engagements than text-only tweets.

Action Plan

Analyze the content and composition of tweets about the alleged last will of Marcos to determine drivers of tweet engagement.

Null Hypotheses

There is no significant difference between the engagement of...

  1. tweets that mention financial institutions and other political personalities and those that do not.
  2. short tweets (short words & fewer characters) and longer tweets that talk about Marcos's alleged last will.
  3. tweets about Marcos's supposed last will that contain media and links and those composed of purely text.

Second: commonalities.

Research Question

What do the tweets with misinformation about Marcos's wealth and last will have in common in terms of:

  1. tweet type,
  2. sentiment of tweet,
  3. year it was tweeted?

Hypotheses

  1. The tweets with misinformation about Marcos's wealth and his supposed last will were more spread through replies and quote tweets.
  2. The tweets were more persuasive rather than aggressive.
  3. The tweets were more frequent during years with elections.

Action Plan

Analyze the tweet type, sentiment, and date of tweets with misinformation about Marcos's wealth and last will to identify the posting patterns of fake news spreaders.

Null Hypotheses

  1. There is no significant difference between the number of original tweets, quote retweets, and replies.
  2. There is no dominant sentiment among tweets regarding Marcos's wealth and last will.
  3. There is no significant increase on the number of tweets regarding Marcos's wealth and last will during the years of election.

Let's talk about our data science methodology.

This is how we preprocessed our data.

Data Cleaning

The timestamps and other relevant dates were converted to Asia/Manila time. New fields: is_image, is_video, is_reply and is_url were created from columns denoting the presence of each. These values were then congregated into 'Tweet Type' which contains a comma delimited list of ['Image', 'Video', 'Reply', 'URL'].

Some columns were dropped, namely: "Link Redirect", "Reviewer", "Review", "Remarks", "Rating", "Reasoning", "Tweet Translated", "Screenshot", "Views"; because they were either extraneous or null columns.

All fields with scraped/entered values were observed for null values using the ydata-profiling package. This allowed us to observe the composition of each feature. From there, we filled null Location and Account bio with empty strings, and engagement metrics (Likes, Retweets, Replies and Quote Retweets) with 0.

In compiling numerical data, we chose to keep outliers as they may be telling of a non-linear trend in the dataset or indicative of a text/thematic feature that has yet to be determined. In keeping the outlier values (especially for engagement), we took careful note of not normalizing, scaling or standardizing data features as this would diminish values possibly belonging to the interquartile range by dividing over the outlier value/s. Because engagement metrics in particular were left-skewed, we chose to leave them unscaled.

For categorical values, the Content type column was one-hot encoded as it is a binary variable. This made it easier to produce time-series charts for this trend. For the Tweet type column, multiple subcolumns (is_reply, is_text, is_link, is_media) were used to encode it as tweets can be combinations of any of these three fields (e.g. tweets containing images and text, or links in reply to the previous tweet, etc.).

Text Feature Generation

The mentions and hashtags columns were created through regex extraction of those elements from the main tweet. The Tweet Translated field was also generated by translating the original tweet text through deep_translator.GoogleTranslator from Filipino to English.

Further preprocessing removed links, punctuation and stop words from the translated tweet. Words were notably not lemmatized as the dimensionality of text features is within the acceptable range. Reducing words to their roots remove crucial information regarding the tense and context (especially for homonyms).

The tweet text itself was also split from the alt-image text in the same field through regex extraction to account for the reading experience of the viewers which directly contribute to engagement.

NLP

The processed tweet content is fed through CountVectorizer and TfidfVectorizer which count each word associated with the tweet per row and perform TF-IDF for more interpretable results. Only the 1-gram is used as tweets do not require as much sequential context. However, 2-3 grams may be further explored.

The TF-IDF processed table was taken as input for Principal Component Analysis. This proved to be inconclusive, however, due to the small sample size. This could, however, be used for developing a machine learning model using these features. It was determined that 83 components can be derived from the 648 words that were part of the dataset to explain its variance.

Data Visualization

First, the data was distributed non-uniformly so we resampled and aggregated each month so that the time series plots would turn out uniform. The following plots were plotted using seaborn, pandas-profiling, matplotlib, and pandas.

Time Series of Fake News Frequencies

In 2016, the number of fake news tweets peaked at nine on June, a month after the 2016 elections. From 2021 onwards, the tweets started spiking again. The tweets peaked at October 2021, the period where Marcos filed for candidacy for the 2022 elections. It could be observed that there is an increase of frequencies of fake news tweets about Marcos’ Last Will during election seasons, those in which Bongbong Marcos is running.


Time Series of Fake News Content Type

During election seasons, there were more rational tweets compared to emotional tweets. It can also be observed that emotional tweets increased in number during the 2022 election season compared to the 2016 election season.


Correlation Heat Map

From the heatmap, we can pick out the relevant pairs of variables with moderate to high correlations: (Following, Followers), (Followers, Quote Tweets), (Likes, Retweets), (Replies, Quote Tweets), (Retweets, Quote Tweets), (Quote Tweets, Following)

Pair Plots

We obtained the variables with correlations from the heat map and plotted them in these pair plots to better visualize their relationship. From the pair plots, we can see that the pairs of variables with a relatively high/strong positive correlation are Following and Followers, and Replies and Quote Tweets. The high positive correlation of Replies and Quote Tweets may indicate that the number of replies and quote tweets are indicators of the good engagement of the fake news tweet.

The pairs of variables with a moderate positive correlation are Followers and Quote Tweets, Following and Quote Tweets, Likes and Retweets, and Retweets and Quote Tweets. This may indicate that the amount of followers and following an account has may dictate the amount of Quote Tweets engagement the fake news tweet receives. Also, Likes and Retweets and Retweets and Quote Tweets are pairs of variables that moderately (and positively) affect each other, indicating that these engagements can be observed to increase together in a fake news tweet.

Which personalities/institutions were mentioned in the dataset?

Research Question 1A

Marcos Sr.'s immediate relatives: Imelda Marcos, his wife, and Marcos Jr., his son, topped the mentions. Imelda was shown as the advocate for the release of the wealth, while Marcos Jr. was portrayed as the one who would be able to claim the alleged wealth for the Filipino people - of course, only upon election.

The 'foundation' varies according to context: for some, it is where the Marcos wealth was stored while for others, it would supposedly be the institution used to distribute the wealth. The 'government', 'bank' and 'legislature' mentions were part of claims to legitimize the origin of the wealth and its due release to the public.

The succeeding mentions until 'politicians' save for 'duterte', 'switzerland' constitute those who stand in opposition to the supposed release of the Marcos fund to the PH Government while the last set of mentions contain personalities used to further craft the Marcos wealth origin story.

Finding: It seems that the Marcos wealth story is perpetrated to associate the remaining Marcos kin to the promise of progress - all the while painting a story through the linked institutions and antagonizing those who stand in opposition to the Marcoses.

What do the tweets with misinformation about Marcos's wealth and last will have in common in terms of year it was tweeted?

Research Question 2C

Finding:
The data was grouped into two groups: election seasons (2016, 2019, 2021, 2022) and non-election seasons (2017, 2018, 2020), and the Independent Samples T-test was used to test whether the two groups are statistically different.

The Shapiro-Wilk test shows that the election seasons group has a p-value of 0.9917 while the non-election seasons has a p-value of 0.8428, suggesting that both groups follow a normal distribution (since both p-values are greater than 0.05). The Independent Samples T-test showed a T-statistic of 1.7318 with a p-value of 0.1438. Since the p-value is greater than 0.05, we conclude that there are no significant difference or increase on the number of fake news tweets during election years.

However, if we only consider presidential elections seasons for the former group, i.e. election seasons (2016, 2021, 2022) and non-election seasons (2017, 2018, 2019, 2020), we found different results. The Shapiro-Wilk test still confirms that both groups follow a normal distribution, with a p-value of 0.8168 and 0.2407, respectively. The Independent Samples T-test showed a T-statistic of 4.2040 and a p-value of 0.0084, which is less than 0.05. This shows that the frequency of misinformation tweets during national elections seasons are statistically and significantly different and higher than regular years.

Note that in 2019, during the midterm elections, Imee Marcos ran as senator. However, there are significantly less tweets during this period, increasing the standard deviation of the former election seasons group, and thus making it statistically insignificant from the former non-elections group. But considering national election seasons only, we can now see the statistical difference from the latter groups. Therefore, this indicates that the increase of tweet frequency could be associated with Bongbong Marcos running for a more powerful position (Vice President in 2016 and President in 2022)--positions that have more power in "fulfilling" the late dictator's last will.
